    • What are non-transitive (intrasitive) games?

    Intrasitive games are games in which there are at least 3 strategies A, B and C. For a game to be non-transitive, it must satisfy the following conditions:
    1. No 2 strategies can be the same (i.e. A cannot equal B)
    2. Suppose strategy A is better than strategy B (A > B)
    3. Suppose also strategy B is better than strategy C (B > C)
    4. It is natural to deduce that if A > B and B > C, then A > C. However, in non-transitive games that is not the case!
    5. Strategies are related by one or more loops
    To illustrate the behaviour of non-transitive games, the games used to experiment will be Rock, Paper, Scissors & Penney's Game
